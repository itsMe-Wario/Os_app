Recap:

What is the use of Dining Philosophers Problem?

The dining Philosopher's Problem is the is needed to in computer science and distributed systems courses to teach concepts related to concurrent programming, synchronization, and avoiding race conditions. It serves as a practical example to explore solutions like semaphores, mutexes, and other synchronization mechanisms in multi-process or multi-threaded environments.

What is the use of binary semaphores in Dining Philosophers Problem?

In the Dining Philosophers Problem, binary semaphores are used to represent chopsticks. Each philosopher must acquire two neighboring chopsticks to eat. Binary semaphores help control access to chopsticks, ensuring exclusive use and preventing conflicts among philosophers. They play a crucial role in designing a solution to avoid deadlock and ensure fair access to resources in a concurrent dining scenario.
